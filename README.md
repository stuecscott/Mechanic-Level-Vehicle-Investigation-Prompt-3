Mechanic-Level Vehicle Investigation Prompt

A professional automotive research prompt built for buyers who care more about durability and failure history than appearance, trim level, or marketing hype.

This prompt is designed to investigate the kinds of issues mechanics, dealership buyers, and auction flippers look for before taking on a vehicle. Instead of asking whether a car is “good,” it asks whether that vehicle has a track record of becoming a financial problem once mileage climbs into the 80,000 to 120,000-mile range.

It focuses on hidden reliability risks such as engine design flaws, timing chain failures, oil consumption problems, CVT breakdown history, recurring repairs, and technician-reported weaknesses that may not be obvious from consumer-facing reviews.

What it does

Examines known engine weaknesses by model, year, engine, and transmission

Looks for timing chain stretch, guide failures, tensioner issues, and cam phaser problems

Investigates oil burning, excessive consumption, ring wear, sludge issues, and PCV-related failures

Detects CVT reliability problems, early transmission failures, overheating, shuddering, and replacement trends

Reviews mechanic repair data, technician discussions, owner failure reports, and complaint patterns

Checks NHTSA complaints, recalls, TSBs, and long-term ownership reports

Separates vehicles with proven drivetrains from vehicles with a polished appearance but poor long-term durability

Produces a practical risk assessment for buying, flipping, or avoiding the vehicle entirely

Best for used vehicle buyers

Auction flippers

Dealership inventory screeners

Mechanics helping customers evaluate purchases

Anyone trying to avoid costly vehicles with delayed failure patterns

Example use cases

“Investigate the 2017 Chevrolet Equinox 2.4L for timing chain and oil consumption issues.”

“Check whether the 2015 Nissan Altima has CVT failure trends past 90k miles.”

“Compare the long-term engine and transmission reliability of a 2018 Ford Escape 1.5L vs. a 2018 Toyota RAV4.”

“Review whether this model is safe to flip or likely to become a comeback vehicle with expensive repairs.”
